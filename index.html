Hello, and welcome to my website!

I am Syed Naveed Mahmood, a Computer Science and Engineering graduate from BRAC University and currently a Contractual Faculty member at Daffodil International University.

My research focuses on trustworthy artificial intelligence, with particular interests in LLM safety, machine unlearning, mechanistic interpretability, representation-level model auditing, secure AI agents, and reliable evaluation. I am especially interested in understanding what models learn internally, why interventions work, and whether their effects remain dependable under adversarial and realistic conditions.

I also work on interpretable machine learning for physiological signals, including EEG sleep-stage classification, transferability across datasets, and clinically meaningful explanations of model predictions.

Previously, I worked as an Undergraduate Teaching Assistant at BRAC University, where I supported laboratory instruction, assessment, and mentoring for more than 100 students.

Current Work

LLM Agent Security

MIRAGE-Persist

Studying how security failures can persist across long-running, tool-using AI systems and how they should be evaluated reliably.

AI Safety · Interpretability

AgentERUF

Exploring representation-aware methods for understanding and defending AI agents against injected or manipulated objectives.

Mechanistic Interpretability · Reproducibility

Attention-Sink Circuits

Reproducing and stress-testing mechanistic explanations of attention sinks across implementations and experimental settings.

Efficient & Reliable Language Models

Activation-Space Model Correction

Investigating whether compact activation-level interventions can improve the reliability of compressed language models.

Trustworthy AI · Model Security

Mixture-of-Experts Robustness

Studying internal model signals that may help detect and mitigate compromised behavior in mixture-of-experts language models.

AI for Health · EEG

MIST-Sleep

Developing interpretable sleep-staging systems that remain dependable across datasets, recording settings, and subject populations.
